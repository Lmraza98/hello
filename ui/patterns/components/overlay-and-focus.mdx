# Overlay and Focus Patterns

This note defines interaction rules for popovers, dialogs, menus, and drawers.

## Open and Close Behavior

- Escape closes the topmost open surface first.
- Click outside closes transient overlays unless the interaction starts from a blocked element.
- Nested overlays must close in reverse open order.

## Focus Management

- On open, focus moves to the first meaningful control or heading anchor.
- On close, focus returns to the element that opened the surface.
- Keep a fallback target when opener element unmounts.

## Click Handling

- Controls inside rows must stop propagation when they launch overlays.
- Do not treat scrollbar drags or text selection as outside clicks.
- Pointer down and click handlers must agree on whether dismissal occurs.

## Dialog and Drawer Rules

- Modal surfaces trap focus while open.
- Non-modal popovers do not trap focus but should maintain logical tab order.
- Body scroll lock applies only to modal dialog and mobile drawer states.

## Accessibility

- Every dismiss control needs an `aria-label` when icon-only.
- Use `aria-expanded`, `aria-controls`, and `aria-haspopup` where relevant.
- Provide descriptive titles for dialogs and drawers so context is clear.
